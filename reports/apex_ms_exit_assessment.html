Top 3 Risks

  • Critical Cybersecurity Gaps Blocking Deal Closure: The absence of a formal documented incident response plan, shared administrative credentials stored without dedicated PAM vaulting (HIGH risk), and lack of recent external penetration testing are explicitly flagged as deal-blocking vulnerabilities that create regulatory exposure for healthcare-adjacent clients and will deter strategic buyers in the MSP space.

  • Unvalidated Financial Quality and Missing Supporting Documentation: The absence of audited financial statements, detailed P&L breakdowns, and historical growth documentation prevents buyer validation of the reported $412K normalized EBITDA and 18% margin, creating deal risk and likely triggering significant buyer pushback during financial due diligence.

Recommended Priority Fixes

Actions the company should take in the next 90 days to maximise exit readiness:

Fix 1
Develop and Document Formal Incident Response Plan (Weeks 1-3): Create a written, board-approved incident response procedure covering detection, containment, eradication, and recovery phases; align with healthcare regulatory requirements (HIPAA if applicable); conduct internal tabletop exercise to validate plan and train staff on roles and escalation protocols.
Fix 2
Implement Dedicated Credential Vaulting and Retire Shared Credentials (Weeks 1-4): Deploy a PAM solution (or BitWarden/1Password team vault) to replace IT Glue credential storage; migrate all shared administrative credentials; document credential rotation schedules and access audit trails; complete internal access review to ensure segregation of duties.
Fix 3
Conduct External Penetration Test and Remediate Findings (Weeks 2-6): Engage a third-party security firm to perform scope-limited pen test ($8K-12K); document and remediate HIGH and CRITICAL findings; prepare remediation evidence package for buyer review to demonstrate proactive security posture.
Fix 4
Build Organizational Chart, Succession Plan, and Key Person Transition Plan (Weeks 1-4): Document formal org structure with role definitions and decision authority; create succession plan for owner-dependent roles (client relationships, vCIO delivery); draft transition services agreement (TSA) template specifying 3-6 month owner transition commitment; identify and train backup for each critical client contact.
Fix 5
Prepare Audited/Reviewed Financial Statements and Supporting Documentation (Weeks 2-8): Engage external accountant to prepare reviewed financial statements for FY 2024-2025 (minimum); compile detailed P&L by service line, customer acquisition cost, and churn analysis; document revenue recognition policies and one-time/non-recurring expenses to support EBITDA normalization claims of $412K.
