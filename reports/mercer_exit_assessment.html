Top 3 Strengths

  • Documented Client Onboarding Process: Mercer Law Partners has a mature, maintained SOP (v1.8) for client intake with clear procedures, timelines, and tool integration, demonstrating baseline operational rigor expected of a professional services firm ready for acquisition.
  • Stable, Recurring Revenue Base: The firm generated $1.025M in FY2025 revenue with demonstrated stability across FY2023-2025 ($816K to $1.025M), primarily anchored in retainer relationships that provide predictable cash flow attractive to buyers.
  • Foundational IT Infrastructure: Clio Manage and NetDocuments are established practice management and document management systems with role-based access controls and SOC 2 compliance, providing a scalable technology backbone for post-acquisition integration.

Top 3 Risks

  • Critical Owner Dependency with No Succession Plan: The sole owner controls conflict checks, client intake, engagement documentation, matter setup, and top-client relationships with zero documented delegation or succession planning (Owner Dependency 4/10), creating material business continuity and valuation risk that will concern any buyer.
  • Material Cybersecurity and Compliance Gaps: Unencrypted client data transmission via email, lack of non-attorney MFA, and absent endpoint detection and response capabilities violate Georgia Rules of Professional Conduct and expose privileged attorney-client communications to breach risk (Cybersecurity 5/10), with potential deal-blocking implications.
  • Undocumented Customer Contract Terms and Transferability: While revenue is stable and retainer-based, critical gaps in documented renewal rates, change-of-control provisions, and contract transferability language (Customer Contracts 4/10) substantially increase buyer risk and create deal certainty issues around revenue retention post-acquisition.

Recommended Priority Fixes

Actions the company should take in the next 90 days to maximise exit readiness:

Fix 1
Weeks 1-4: Execute formal succession and delegation plan documenting transition of the owner's critical functions (conflict checks, client intake, engagement documentation) to identified attorney(s); include written protocols, sign-off authority, and training milestones to begin demonstrating business continuity to potential buyers.
Fix 2
Weeks 1-3: Remediate cybersecurity gaps by enabling MFA for all non-attorney staff, implementing encrypted email or secure client portal for document transmission, and deploying endpoint detection and response (EDR) on firm devices—estimated cost under $3,000 one-time plus $200/month, with completion before buyer due diligence.
Fix 3
Weeks 2-6: Conduct comprehensive customer contract audit to document renewal terms, change-of-control language, transferability rights, and expiration dates for all retainer relationships; create standardized contract template with M&A-friendly provisions (e.g., explicit change-of-control consent waiver or notice requirement) for future engagements.
Fix 4
Weeks 3-8: Formalize data room structure and document inventory, including all SOPs (cybersecurity, data handling, attorney transition protocols per Georgia Rules of Professional Conduct), IT asset registry, customer contracts with renewal/transfer analysis, financial statements (FY2023-2025), and organizational charts reflecting succession documentation.
Fix 5
Weeks 4-12: Implement or document formalized CRM/pipeline tracking process in Clio Manage or dedicated system to capture sales activity, deal stages, pipeline forecasting, and activity metrics—creating visibility into sales execution and revenue pipeline that is currently absent and required for buyer confidence in financial trajectory post-acquisition.
