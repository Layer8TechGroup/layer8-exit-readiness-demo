⏱ Suggested 30-Minute Demo Flow

0–5 min
Context Setting
Lead with the problem, not the product. Ask: "How do your clients typically find out how ready they are before you start a process?" Let them describe the gap. Then: "We built a tool that answers that question before you pick up the phone with a buyer." Keep it under 90 seconds. Don't demo yet.
5–10 min
Show the Portfolio Summary
Open index.html. Walk them through the score distribution — note that only 3 of 12 are near-ready or better. Ask: "In your current book, what percentage do you think would be above a 6.5?" Let them answer. The portfolio exists to mirror what a real book looks like — not cherry-picked. Emphasize the EBITDA and implied value columns: "This is what each company is leaving on the table right now."
10–20 min
Drill Into 2–3 Company Reports
Suggested sequence: Meridian (show what good looks like — 8.3, one gap, document away from done), then Cornerstone (mid-range, good ops but one concentration flag — relatable), then Pinnacle (low score, strong EBITDA — the "valuable but not listable" scenario every broker knows). Skip the Detailed Findings on the first pass — go straight to the Value Recovery Roadmap.
20–25 min
Focus: Value Recovery Roadmap
This is the key section. Show the Pinnacle roadmap: $1,296,000 in recoverable value across 5 services, 3 months timeline, dollar-quantified. Say: "Every one of those line items is a service Layer8 delivers. We're not handing them a list — we're handing them a project plan." Let that land. Then show the timeline column: "8–10 weeks for the biggest items. Most of this is completable before you've drafted the CIM."
25–30 min
The Ask
Read the room (see Ask Options below). Default opening: the pilot. Make it zero-risk: one client, no cost to the broker, full report in 5 business days. If they're warm, move to the referral model conversation. If the room is cold, leave the Framework Guide and ask for a follow-up.
Best companies to demo by audience type: Healthcare broker → Meridian. Commercial broker → Cornerstone or Blue Collar. PE-adjacent → Peachtree or Halcyon. Skeptic who needs the "before" picture → Pinnacle.

💬 Likely Objections & Responses

"How is this different from what I already do as a broker?"
"You do the qualitative version — you read the business and form a view. We do the quantitative version — we score it across five domains with evidence from the company's own documents, and we put a dollar figure on each gap before you've had one buyer conversation. You keep doing what you do; this just gives you better data going in."
"My clients won't share this information with a third party."
"They share it with you. We work as an extension of your engagement — documents stay in your data room, we access only what you and the client authorize, and we operate under your existing NDA with the client. From the client's perspective, you brought in a specialist, not a third party."
"What does this cost?"
"Assessment only is $1,500 per company — less than a half-day of your time to produce a document that could add $200K to the close price. If the client wants the remediation plan, that's $3,500 all-in. Full remediation engagement is scoped per domain — typically $15K–$50K depending on how many gaps need closing. The assessment pays for itself the first time a buyer doesn't re-trade at LOI."
"Who actually does the remediation work?"
"Layer8 delivers it directly. We're not a referral service — we have practitioners who do succession planning documentation, data room build, cybersecurity hardening, CRM implementation, and books cleanup. You refer the client; we deliver the service; you get a better-prepared listing. We don't compete with you for the M&A advisory relationship."
"What if the score is wrong?"
"Every score cites the specific document that generated it — it's not a black box. If a client says 'that's not right,' we look at the source. Nine times out of ten, the score is right and the document they meant to provide wasn't in the data room — which is itself a finding. Occasionally a score needs recalibration when context is added. We stand behind the methodology and we'll revisit any finding that's challenged with new evidence."
"I don't want to add friction to my intake process."
"Neither do we. The intake is a standard document checklist — the same materials you'd collect for the CIM anyway: financials, contracts, org chart, basic IT info. We turn around the report in 5 business days. You get a scored assessment before you've written a paragraph of the CIM. That's not friction — that's a head start."

🎯 The Ask — Choose Based on Room Temperature

🌡 Warm Room
"Would you be open to running one client through this as a pilot — no cost to you? You pick the company, we deliver the report in 5 business days, you keep the output regardless of what happens next."
Best for: engaged brokers who asked good questions during the demo. Zero-risk entry point.
💰 Standard Ask
"We're building a network of 3–5 broker partners in Atlanta to co-develop the referral model. 15% of assessment and remediation fees, introduced clients only. Want to be part of that conversation?"
Best for: brokers with active books who see the revenue angle. Opens a partnership conversation.
❌ Cold Room
"Can I leave the Framework Guide with you and follow up next week? Sometimes this lands differently after you've had a client conversation where it would have been useful."
Best for: skeptical or distracted rooms. Plant the seed, don't force it. Follow up in 7 days.
Never leave without a next step. Even "cold room" has a follow-up date. Offer to send the portfolio link so they can explore between meetings — it's a better leave-behind than a PDF.
